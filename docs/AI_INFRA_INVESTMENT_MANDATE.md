# AI Infra Investment Mandate

This document is the investment mandate behind the pipeline changes. It is not
a factor spec and not a prompt template.

The upstream research workbench lives in `ai_infra/`. That directory is the
canonical target for the pipeline migration away from broad all-market scanning.
It owns the LLM-dependency BFS framework, global AI-infra universe, source
verification queues, evidence-card templates, US alpha mining queue and
public/private data boundary.

The local summary map lives in `docs/AI_INFRA_RESEARCH_MAINLINES.md`. It mirrors
the main AI-infra supply-chain research themes for the production pipeline:
HBM, CoWoS, CPO/optical, rack-scale connectivity, custom ASIC, NeoCloud, power,
cooling, storage, silicon photonics, materials and the AI-infra bear-case
dashboard.

The first detailed source-review-gated memo is
`docs/AI_INFRA_HBM_COWOS_DEEP_DIVE.md`. It covers the HBM structural-standard
thesis and the CoWoS / advanced-packaging bottleneck map. It is a research
input until primary sources are checked, not a source-confirmed relationship
ledger.

## Core View

The portfolio should be built around the AI infrastructure supercycle. The goal
is not to scan the whole market every day and buy whatever looks statistically
interesting. The goal is to participate in the strongest technological capital
cycle available: model scale, cloud distribution, accelerator compute, memory,
networking, datacenter infrastructure, semiconductor supply chain, power and
space-connectivity adjacencies.

AI is the frontier technology stack. Silicon intelligence has a credible path
to exponential demand, while many older sectors are cyclical, slow, policy
dependent, or structurally weak. The pipeline should therefore start from an
AI-infra universe and use quant signals to decide timing, not start from the
entire market and hope the ranker rediscovers the theme.

## Universe First

The first filter is mandate fit:

- Model, cloud and application distribution.
- AI labs and model-platform owners.
- CPU/GPU/TPU, custom accelerators, AI servers and foundry interfaces.
- HBM/DRAM/NAND, storage and data infrastructure.
- CPO, optical modules, fiber, datacenter networking and internet delivery.
- Semiconductor equipment, packaging, testing, substrates and materials.
- Datacenter power, cooling, grid, nuclear and electrification.
- Space communication, commercial space and future orbital data infrastructure.
- Hard-asset inputs only when they directly support the AI capex cycle.

Within that universe, the first research priority is the intersection of
`HBM/CoWoS equipment-test-materials`, `optical/CPO`, `power/liquid cooling`,
`custom ASIC connectivity`, and `non-US hidden supply-chain champions`.

Daily consumption, old consumer brands and unrelated traditional sectors are
not core hunting grounds. Railways, consumer staples and other old-economy
stories need either a separately proven sleeve or a direct AI-infra linkage
before they deserve attention.

## Signal Philosophy

The pipeline should follow strength inside the right universe.

For A-shares, news is often late. Price, volume, flow, sector breadth and
relative leadership are the first signal. News is a lagging risk/evidence label,
not the reason to enter.

For US equities, price, news/event evidence and options/flow can be used
together. Options are decision evidence for stock trades, not a separate reason
to avoid the equity call.

The preferred trade is right-side theme leadership. Left-side value/oversold is
allowed only when the company belongs to the mandate and the expected-value
bucket is historically proven. A cheap or oversold non-AI stock is not enough.

## Evidence Discipline

Theme membership is a prior, not proof. A company can be in an AI layer because
its industry or product category fits, but supplier/customer claims require
source-linked evidence.

The pipeline must distinguish:

- `theme_prior`: belongs to an AI-infra layer.
- `price_flow_first_no_current_news`: tape confirms interest, but no direct
  relationship proof yet.
- `source_linked_supply_evidence`: filings, official releases, transcripts or
  credible news confirm the relationship.
- `research_only`: long-horizon watch item, no same-day R.

The aim is to find bottlenecks that can become large value-investment winners:
capacity constraints, materials, packaging, optical bandwidth, power, grid
interconnect, memory bandwidth and infrastructure scarcity. The pipeline should
surface these candidates early, then force source review before turning the
story into an investment reason.

## What Is Not Core

The industrial AI-lab publication index is optional. It can help describe the
quality of model/cloud companies, but it is not the center of the strategy and
must not block the broader AI-infra pipeline.

The core pipeline shift is more important:

1. Restrict the search space to AI infra and direct adjacencies.
2. Rank by theme strength, tape/flow, evidence quality and risk-adjusted setup.
3. Separate research hypotheses from source-confirmed relationships.
4. Promote only names with mandate fit plus tradeable price behavior.
5. Maintain beta hedge and residual risk attribution at portfolio level.
6. Keep US and A-share reports separate, human-readable and free of prompt
   boilerplate.

## Pipeline Contract

Every report should answer four questions:

1. Which AI-infra layers are leading now?
2. Which stocks inside those layers have price/flow confirmation?
3. Which supplier or bottleneck claims are source-confirmed versus still
   research-only?
4. What is the trade plan, hedge, invalidation and residual risk?

If a candidate cannot answer those questions, it may stay in research watch, but
it should not consume execution R.
